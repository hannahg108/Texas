Top Five Must Visit Landmarks In Texas

1. State Capitol - Austin

The new capital was named for Stephen F. Austin known as the Father of Texas but many people including Sam Houston were dissatisfied with the capitol's location. Houston and others were worried about the remoteness of the site fearing it would be hard to defend from the Mexican army and local Indians.


2. The Alamo — San Antonio

The Battle of the Alamo was fought between the Republic of Texas and Mexico from February 23, 1836 to March 6, 1836. It took place at a fort in San Antonio, Texas called the Alamo. The Mexicans won the battle, killing all of the Texan soldiers inside the fort.


3. Palo Duro Canyon — Canyon

Palo Duro Canyon was one of the final hideouts for Comanche Indians led by War Chief Quanah Parker during the Red River War (1874-1875), a U.S. military campaign to remove Native Americans from the Southern Plains and relocate them to reservations in Oklahoma


4. Big Bend National Park — West Texas

The best time of year to visit Big Bend National Park is the spring or the fall. The desert is a land of extremes: Summer brings heat that often reaches 100 degrees, and winter brings snow and temperatures that dip into the 30s.


5. Mountain Guadalupe

It is rated strenuous, with 3,000 feet of elevation gain. The round trip distance is 8.4 miles, and generally takes 6-8 hours.
